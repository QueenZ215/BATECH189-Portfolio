In the early 2000s, BP hired a major PR firm, Ogilvy & Mather, to help divert attention away from its corporate responsibility for climate change. That’s when the term ’carbon footprint’ really took off, not as a scientific tool but as a way to suggest that everyday people were the ones driving the problem. While the fossil fuel industry continued to operate at full throttle, the public was subtly encouraged to feel guilty about their commutes and coffee cups.
